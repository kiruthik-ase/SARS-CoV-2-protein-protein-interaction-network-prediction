About

Project Overview

A computational approach to understand how SARS-CoV-2 hijacks human cellular machinery.

🦠

The Problem

SARS-CoV-2 interacts with thousands of human proteins to replicate and evade immune responses. Mapping these interactions experimentally is slow and expensive.

🤖

Our Approach

We trained an XGBoost classifier on experimentally validated interactions from BioGRID, using protein sequence composition as features to predict novel interactions.

🎯

Impact

Our model can rapidly screen potential viral–human interactions, prioritizing candidates for experimental validation and accelerating antiviral drug discovery.

Methodology

Data Pipeline

End-to-end workflow from raw biological databases to trained prediction model.

01

Data Collection

Experimentally validated SARS-CoV-2 × Human interactions from BioGRID v5.0.251 (COVID-19 project). Protein sequences from UniProt and NCBI RefSeq.

32,250 positive interactions 14 viral proteins
02

Negative Sampling

Random pairing of viral × human proteins not in the positive set. 1:1 ratio balanced dataset to ensure unbiased training.

32,250 negatives ~64,500 total pairs
03

Feature Extraction

For each protein: Amino Acid Composition (20), Dipeptide Composition (400), Physicochemical properties (5) via Biopython.

425 features per protein 850 per pair
04

Model Training

XGBoost gradient boosted trees (600 estimators, max_depth=8, lr=0.05). Evaluated via stratified split, human-wise split, and leave-one-viral-out CV.

XGBoost classifier 3 evaluation strategies
Feature Engineering

850 Sequence-Based Features

Protein sequences are encoded into numerical vectors capturing composition and physicochemical properties.

Amino Acid Composition

20 per protein

Frequency of each of the 20 standard amino acids (A, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, K, L, M, N, P, Q, R, S, T, V, W, Y).

AAC(a) = count(a) / L

Dipeptide Composition

400 per protein

Frequency of all 20×20 = 400 possible consecutive amino acid pairs, capturing local sequence patterns.

DPC(ab) = count(ab) / (L−1)

Physicochemical Properties

5 per protein

Sequence length, molecular weight, GRAVY (hydrophobicity), aromaticity, and instability index via Biopython.

Computed via ProteinAnalysis
